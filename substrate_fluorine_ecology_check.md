**1.1 A Note on Classification: Why "Plant" Is Misleading**

**[NEW IN V2.4]**

The term "thorn-plant" is a convenience. These organisms are not plants
in any meaningful Earth sense. The plant/animal distinction collapses
completely in this biosphere:

**No photosynthesis.** Earth plants use light to drive chemical synthesis
(carbon fixation via chlorophyll). Thorn-flowers do no chemistry with
light at all. They run parabolic-mirror concentrated solar → semiconductor
photovoltaic conversion → charge storage in root-capacitors. The energy
product is *electricity*, not synthesized organic molecules. They are
solar power stations, not photosynthesizers.

**No autotroph/heterotroph divide.** On Earth, autotrophs synthesize
organic molecules from light or chemical energy; heterotrophs eat
autotrophs. On this planet, every organism runs on the same currency:
electrical charge. Thorn-flowers harvest charge from the sun. Tower
fauna tap the lattice grid. Vipers tap thorn-flowers. Amorphs metabolize
atmospheric SiH₄. Different sources, same currency. There is no chemical
synthesis step that separates "producers" from "consumers."

**The actual organizing axes of this biosphere:**

| Axis | Distinction |
|---|---|
| **Phylogeny** | Meme-based (amorph: fluid, merge, meme library) vs. genome-based (sexual lineage: rigid, real genome, sexual reproduction). The deepest split. |
| **Energy source** | Tower-coupled (grid-tapping) vs. tower-independent (solar-electric, SiH₄ metabolism, predation, scavenging). The primary *ecological* divide. |
| **Motility** | Sessile vs. mobile. Exists within both lineages. Most tower fauna are sessile. Thorn-flowers are sessile. Vipers are mobile. |
| **Tissue chemistry** | Silazane (default, HF-vulnerable) vs. silane (specialist, HF-immune). Cuts across all other axes. |

The thorn-flowers are: sessile, genome-based, tower-independent,
solar-electric, silazane-tissue organisms. Calling them "plants" imports
a framework (photosynthesis, autotrophy, producer trophic level) that
does not apply. They are closer to sessile animals with solar panels
than to anything a botanist would recognize.

The term "thorn-plant" is retained throughout these documents as
convenient shorthand. Do not read taxonomy into it.

**2. Chemistry Foundations**

**2.1 The HF Weapon System**

*\[CONFIRMED --- V2.3\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** Fluorine cannot be stored in elemental or HF form in
biological tissue. It must be stored as CaF₂ (fluorite), chemically
inert until deliberately activated.

**✓ RESOLVED:** The weapon is a two-compartment system analogous to the
bombardier beetle: CaF₂ crystallites in one chamber, strong Brønsted
acid in another, separated by a membrane. When the compartments mix:
CaF₂ + acid → HF generated in situ.

**✓ RESOLVED:** HF attacks Si-N bonds in silazane tissue. Tissue
dissolves. SiF₄ gas escapes as byproduct. The wound hisses.

**2.2 Tissue Chemistry Bifurcation**

*\[CONFIRMED --- V2.3\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** The ecosystem bifurcates into two metabolic strategies
based on tissue chemistry:

**Silazane organisms (majority)**: Standard tissue chemistry with Si-N
bonds. Vulnerable to HF attack. Can safely store CaF₂ as passive defense
--- predators that eat them get acid + fluorite mixing in their gut,
generating HF internally. Poisonous to eat. Aposematic warning signals.

**Silane organisms (specialist minority)**: Tissue based on Si-H, Si-Si,
Si-C bonds. Immune to HF. Can eat fluorite-rich prey without
consequence. Can deploy HF weapons without self-damage. Major
biochemical reorganization required. Adopted only where the tradeoff
pays.

**✓ RESOLVED:** Silazane is the default strategy --- metabolically
simpler, works 99.99% of the time. Silane is a specialist strategy,
convergently evolved in multiple predator lineages that specialize in
'toxic' prey.

**3. Thorn-Plants**

**3.1 Distribution and Metabolism**

*\[REVISED --- V2.4 --- geothermal primary, solar derived\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** CaF₂ (fluorite) deposits form naturally around volcanic
vents. The ancestral thorn-flower evolved at these vents, rooting into
volcanic substrate for both geothermal energy (thermoelectric roots,
§3.3) and fluorite mining. The same organism, same substrate, same
roots — energy and ammunition from one source.

The plants can only grow armed thorns where fluorite is available. Away
from volcanic vents, a derived solar-powered variant grows with ceramic
thorns only (mechanical defense, not chemical). See §3.3 for the
dual-source energy model and §3.6 for variant architectures.

**3.2 Thorn Architecture**

**✓ RESOLVED:** The thorns are two-compartment structures: SiC-walled
vesicles containing CaF₂ crystallites, separated by a membrane from a
reservoir of strong Brønsted acid.

Passive defense: anything that bites the plant gets stabbed. The thorn
breaks off in the attacker's tissue. Internal divider ruptures. Acid
contacts fluorite. HF generates in situ. The wound hisses. Standard
passive defense for a sessile organism.

**✓ RESOLVED:** Every thorn is a reproductive propagule. The SiC
structure contains an embryonic seed. Armed thorns disperse both the
seed and the CaF₂ weapon chemistry; unarmed thorns disperse seeds
only. The defensive function and the reproductive function are the same
structure: "my offspring are weapons."

**✓ RESOLVED:** Thorns grow on **dedicated thorn bodies** —
specialized fruiting/defense structures that sprout at strategic
locations around the organism, primarily protecting root-capacitor
storage organs (§3.6). Each thorn body is a squat SiC stalk (3–5 cm
tall for a mature specimen) topped with a dense cluster of thorns at
various maturity stages — newest at center, oldest (ready to detach) at
periphery. Charging pads are integrated into or adjacent to thorn
bodies. To charge, a customer must engage with the thorns. That is the
deal: electricity for dispersal.

**3.3 Energy Harvesting: Dual-Source Model**

*\[REVISED --- V2.4 --- geothermal primary, solar derived\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** The thorn-flowers are not connected to the tower grid.
They generate their own power, completely independent of the tower
network. No parasitism, no tolerance required, no relationship with the
grid at all. Energy is stored in root-capacitors.

**✓ RESOLVED: Geothermal (ancestral, armed variant).** The original
thorn-flower evolved near volcanic vents — the same substrate that
provides CaF₂. Deep roots reach into volcanically heated rock.
Thermoelectric conversion via Seebeck effect in semiconductor tissue
(a physical property of silicon — not something that needs to be
"evolved," just optimized). Temperature gradient between hot root tips
and cool surface drives voltage directly. No mirrors, no optics, no
concentration geometry. The simplest possible engineering for a sessile
silicon organism on volcanic substrate.

**✓ RESOLVED: Solar (derived, unarmed variant).** As the species
radiated away from vent zones, geothermal energy attenuated. Selection
pressure drove evolution of the reflector canopy — an array of small
rigid SiC panels with metal silicide mirror coating, arranged as a
heliostat array concentrating light onto a central PV organ (§3.6).
Solar PV is a later, more complex adaptation. The unarmed population
runs on sunlight because they have no volcanic heat to tap.

**✓ RESOLVED: Dual-source (day-side vent zones).** Where volcanism
and insolation overlap, thorn-flowers use both energy sources. Premium
real estate: geothermal provides baseload power independent of star
angle; solar supplements during clear conditions.

The thorn-flower's power infrastructure is entirely separate from the
towers. Two independent energy systems on the same planet, serving
different ecological niches.

**3.4 Pollination via Charging**

*\[CONFIRMED --- V2.3 --- universal pollination mechanism\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** Customers charge from conductive pads adjacent to the
thorn bodies (§3.6). To reach the charging surfaces, customers must
navigate through mature thorns — and in doing so, pick them up
(passively as cockleburrs, or deliberately in the viper case). Thorns
carry pollen from their parent plant. When a customer carrying thorns
from Plant A visits Plant B, the hitchhiking thorn contacts Plant B's
receptive tissue → cross-pollination. The thorn is simultaneously
seed, pollen vehicle, and defense: triple function.

**✓ RESOLVED:** This is the pollination mechanism for the entire
species. Any organism that regularly visits thorn-flowers to charge, and
visits multiple flowers, is a pollinator. The thorns do the work. No
separate pollination organ needed — the defensive/reproductive
structure IS the pollen delivery system.

Like bees carrying pollen on their legs between flowers, but the pollen
is a weaponized seed and the nectar is electricity.

**Universal Mechanism**

**✓ RESOLVED:** The charging-station pollination works everywhere
thorn-flowers grow --- armed and unarmed alike. Wherever there's
sunlight and soil, thorn-flowers can establish and attract pollinators.

**✓ RESOLVED:** The unarmed population (away from vents) is not
'defenseless thorn-flowers waiting to get lucky.' They're running a
successful pollination-via-charging business across the entire species
range. Ceramic thorns provide mechanical defense. Solar harvesting
attracts pollinators. This is a viable life strategy independent of
fluorine chemistry.

The armed population (near vents) has the same solar-powered
pollination, PLUS CaF₂ access enabling chemical weapons, PLUS the viper
mutualism as a specialized dispersal mechanism.

**Vipers as Specialized Pollinators**

**✓ RESOLVED:** The vipers are pollinators of the armed thorn-flowers
specifically. They're local to volcanic vent regions because they need
ammunition --- the armed thorns. Power is available from any
thorn-flower. It's the CaF₂ that constrains their range.

**✓ RESOLVED:** Vipers visit multiple armed thorn-flowers to charge,
contacting reproductive organs each time. When they make kills,
thorn-seeds are aboard. The viper mutualism provides both pollination
and targeted dispersal to nutrient beds near CaF₂ deposits.

But the armed thorn-flowers don't depend on vipers alone. Other charging
customers also pollinate. The vipers are the dispersal specialists;
general pollination is handled by the broader customer base.

**Diverse Customer Base**

Any organism that needs to charge can use thorn-flowers: small
predators, herbivores, anything with biological capacitors. They all
pollinate by visiting multiple flowers. The thorn-flower has a
diversified customer base that doesn't depend on any single species.

**✓ RESOLVED:** The thorn-flower species is everywhere there's sunlight
and suitable soil. A planet-spanning solar infrastructure, with the
armed vent-zone population as a specialized regional variant. The
fluorine ecology is just one corner of this broader system.

**Electrochromic Charge Display**

*\[CONFIRMED --- V2.3 --- from chemistry thread\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** The leaves advertise charge state through semiconductor
electrochromism. No separate signaling system required --- the signal is
a direct physical consequence of charge state. The leaves can't help it.
Their physical state is visible.

In semiconductors, optical properties change with carrier concentration.
A charged capacitor has high carrier density; a depleted one has low.
This affects reflectivity (high carriers → more metallic), color
(bandgap shifts via Burstein-Moss effect), and IR signature (free
carriers absorb infrared differently).

**✓ RESOLVED:** The display: bright, reflective, metallic sheen when
fully charged. Dark, matte, absorptive finish when depleted. The
transition is continuous as charge builds or drains.

**Field Rhythm**

One viper (or equivalent large customer) drains a plant's capacitors.
The leaves go dark. Over hours, they brighten as the parabolic focus
pumps charge back into the roots. The field has a slow flicker ---
plants cycling through discharge and recharge as customers come and go.

A pollinator scanning a field sees which plants are lit up and which are
dark. It routes toward the bright ones. No wasted trips to empty
stations. Regular customers might develop routes timed to each plant's
recharge rate.

**Dual-Use Signal**

**✓ RESOLVED:** Pollinators use the display to find full stations.
Vipers use the same display to find active ambush sites --- bright
leaves mean pollinators will be visiting. Dark leaves mean nobody's
coming; the ambush site is cold.

The viper reads the same signal the pollinator does, but for opposite
purposes. The thorn-flower doesn't care who's reading. It's not
'sending' a signal. It's just being a charged semiconductor.

**✓ RESOLVED:** No metabolic cost. The plant isn't spending energy to
signal. The display is free --- a side effect of how charged
semiconductors look. Evolution never needed to 'invent' this. It's just
physics.

**3.5 Dispersal Strategies**

**✓ RESOLVED:** Thorn-plants have two dispersal strategies depending on
location:

**Armed flowers (near vents)**: Vipers harvest thorns, shoot prey
nearby, thorn-seeds land in corpses proximate to CaF₂ deposits.
Germination in nutrient bed with CaF₂ access → armed offspring. The
viper mutualism provides targeted dispersal to optimal germination
sites. Seeds are injected into a viable food source that instantly dies
--- no waiting for the host to eventually croak.

**All flowers (everywhere)**: Unarmed thorns function as cockleburrs ---
attach to passing fauna, wait for host to die (electrical activity
ceases → germination trigger). Land wherever the host falls. Grow with
ceramic thorns (mechanical defense). If roots happen to reach CaF₂
deposits, metabolic pathway opens. This is the baseline dispersal that
works across the entire range.

**✓ RESOLVED:** The armed population gets an advantage: instant delivery
to a nutrient bed in the right location. The unarmed population relies
on cockleburr strategies with time and uncertainty as middlemen. Both
work; the armed variant is just more efficient for the vent-zone niche.

**3.6 Organism Architecture: The Federated Design**

**[NEW IN V2.4]**

The thorn-flower is a federated organism with specialized organs for
each function. Two variant architectures exist, reflecting the
evolutionary sequence (geothermal → solar, §3.3):

**GEOTHERMAL VARIANT (ancestral, armed, vent-zone)**

The original thorn-flower. Simpler architecture — no optics at all:

| Organ | Function |
|---|---|
| **Deep thermoelectric roots** | Reach into volcanically heated rock. Seebeck-effect voltage generation across temperature gradient. Also mine CaF₂ from volcanic substrate. |
| **Root-capacitor bank** | Energy storage (underground). The treasure that attracts customers. |
| **Thorn bodies** (×5–8) | Defense + reproduction + charge delivery. Squat SiC stalks (3–5 cm), clustered thorns, conductive charging pads. |
| **Surface body** | Electrochromic display (automatic — charged semiconductor physics). Vascular hub connecting roots to thorn bodies. |

Visually: a low, spiny mound on volcanic rock near a vent. 10–20 cm
across, 5–10 cm tall. Thorn bodies and charging pads, no canopy. Glows
bright when charged, dark when depleted. The simplest possible
architecture for a sessile silicon organism on volcanic substrate.

**SOLAR VARIANT (derived, unarmed, widespread)**

The later, more complex form. Adds optical harvesting for areas without
geothermal heat:

| Organ | Function |
|---|---|
| **Root-capacitor bank** | Energy storage, structural anchor. No deep thermoelectric roots (no volcanic heat to tap). |
| **Thorn bodies** (×5–8) | Defense + reproduction + charge delivery. Identical architecture to geothermal variant. Unarmed thorns (ceramic, no CaF₂). |
| **PV organ** | Central Si semiconductor junction receiving concentrated light. Dark, absorptive surface. |
| **Reflector canopy** | Array of small rigid SiC shells (3–8 cm each), metal silicide mirror inner surface (TiSi₂ or FeSi₂), arranged as heliostat. Outer face: electrochromic display. |

The reflector canopy is NOT cantilevered leaves. Each panel is a small,
rigid, self-supporting curved shell. The panels form a heliostat
array — many small mirrors aimed approximately at the central PV organ.
Solar power tower, not parabolic dish. Biological growth produces "many
small mirrors aimed at center" (simple tropism). Concentration ratio:
3–8×. The canopy is the cheapest organ — no stored energy, easy to
regrow. Defense concentrates at the thorn bodies.

**DUAL-SOURCE (day-side vent zones)**

Where volcanism and insolation overlap, thorn-flowers can develop both
root systems. Deep thermoelectric roots + reflector canopy + armed
thorns. The premium configuration.

**✓ RESOLVED:** Growth sequence (solar variant):

1. **Seedling** (~1 cm): Central body only. No reflectors. Runs on
   atmospheric SiH₄. Establishes root system.
2. **Juvenile** (~3 cm body): First ring of 3–5 reflector scales (~2 cm
   each). First PV organ. Begins generating surplus. Too small to
   attract customers.
3. **Subadult** (~5 cm body): 8–12 panels in 1–2 rings. Effective
   aperture ~15 cm. Produces 1–3 W. First thorn bodies develop. First
   customers. First reproductive capability.
4. **Mature** (~8 cm body): 15–30 panels in 2–3 rings. Effective
   aperture 25–40 cm. Generates 5–15 W. Full charging station. Active
   pollination traffic. 5–8 thorn bodies.
5. **Old growth**: 40+ panels. Outer ring panels lose reflective quality
   → become pure thorn-bearing defensive rampart. Effective aperture
   ~50 cm. Growth rings visible: innermost panels oldest, outermost
   newest.

**✓ RESOLVED:** Growth sequence (geothermal variant): Similar staging
but without the reflector development. Root deepening replaces canopy
expansion as the primary growth investment. Thorn body count increases
with root energy budget. Mature specimens may be smaller above-ground
(10–20 cm footprint) but have extensive sub-surface root networks.

**✓ RESOLVED:** Size budget (mature solar variant):

| Component | Size | Mass |
|---|---|---|
| Reflector canopy | 25–40 cm effective aperture | 0.3–0.8 kg |
| PV organ | 3–5 cm diameter | ~50 g |
| Thorn bodies (×5–8) | 3–5 cm tall each | ~20 g each |
| Root system | 30–50 cm spread underground | 0.5–2 kg |
| **Total** | **30–50 cm footprint, 8–12 cm height** | **1–4 kg** |

Size budget (mature geothermal variant): Smaller above-ground (10–20 cm
footprint, 5–10 cm height, 0.3–1 kg above-ground). Larger below-ground
(deep thermoelectric roots into hot rock, total root mass 1–5 kg).

A squat, ground-hugging organism. More like a sea urchin with a
metallic canopy than anything a botanist would recognize.

**3.8 Range and Distribution**

**[NEW IN V2.4]**

**✓ RESOLVED:** Thorn-flowers are physically viable wherever volcanic
heat or sunlight is available — including the terminator and
potentially dark-side volcanic vents. Their actual range is determined
by ecology, not physics:

**Terminator exclusion (ecological, not physical):** Geothermal
thorn-flowers COULD grow at terminator volcanic vents — the physics
works and the thermal gradient is actually optimal there. But the
towers won't tolerate the competition. An independent charging station
offering free electricity to grid-dependent organisms is a direct
threat to the tower network's energy-gradient control system. The
lattice ecology suppresses competitors naturally: Guard caste attacks
foreign organisms, lattice overgrows nearby sessile competitors, energy
gradients pull mobile fauna toward the grid and away from independents.
The terminator is tower territory. Thorn-flowers are excluded by
ecological competition, not by inability to survive.

**Consequence for terminator farming (§4.4, Warfare Hub §4.4):**
Cultivated thorn-flowers at the terminator require PROTECTION from the
tower ecology — the farmer must shield the crop from lattice
competition. The farming problem is as much about keeping the towers
away as keeping the vipers out.

**Day-side dominance:** The day side is thorn-flower territory because
there are no towers. Day-side towers are a rejected concept (physics
doesn't work). With no lattice competitor, thorn-flowers are the
dominant sessile power infrastructure across the entire day side.

**Substellar edge:** Conditions worsen progressively toward the
substellar point — increasing surface temperature, increasing volcanic
resurfacing frequency, eventually unsurvivable on the ground even with
extensive adaptations. □ OPEN: exact substellar viability boundary.

**Dark-side vents (potential, unexploited):** Geothermal thorn-flowers
could theoretically colonize dark-side volcanic vents. Barriers are
dispersal (surface wind flows dark→day, carrying seeds the wrong way)
and tower competition at dark-side vents. □ OPEN: do any thorn-flowers
reach dark-side vents?

**Day-side wind profile (Bible §5.2):** Surface winds DECAY past the
terminator:

| Zone | Wind (m/s) | Dynamic pressure (Pa) |
|---|---|---|
| Substellar region | ~1–3 (variable, thermal chimney) | 4–37 |
| Mid day-side (40–50° from substellar) | 2–4 | 16–66 |
| Near terminator (20–30° from substellar) | 5–8 | 103–262 |
| Terminator | 9.5 | 370 |

The deep day side is dramatically calmer than the terminator. This is
what makes the solar variant's ground-hugging canopy viable.

**✓ RESOLVED:** Solar variant morphology varies with star angle (tidally
locked = star position fixed = organism shape locked to local stellar
elevation):

**Near substellar (θ = 0–20°) — THE BOWL:** Reflector canopy faces
straight up. Shallow bowl sitting directly on the ground. Height
essentially zero. Wind passes over the low profile. Fields of metallic
bowls, some gleaming, some dark.

**Mid day-side (θ = 30–60°) — THE TILTED CUP:** Canopy canted toward
the star. Asymmetric wedge-base supports the tilt (no stem — squat
ramp, compressive-loaded). Every organism in a field faces the same
immovable star.

**Near terminator (θ = 70–85°) — GEOTHERMAL ONLY:** Solar variant
absent (atmospheric absorption kills insolation, wind loading too
high for near-vertical mirrors). If thorn-flowers exist here at all,
they are geothermal-only, canopy-less, low-profile mounds — and likely
suppressed by tower competition unless deliberately cultivated.

**3.9 Dispersal Outcome Matrix**

**[NEW IN V2.4]**

**✓ RESOLVED:** A viper-launched thorn has a dispersal matrix where
every outcome is a reproductive win:

| Outcome | Range | Germination quality | Habitat quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Penetrates target, target dies** | Short (local) | **Excellent** — nutrient bed, near vent = armed offspring | Optimal |
| **Penetrates, target survives** | Medium (carried) | Good — germinates when host eventually dies | Variable but viable |
| **Bounces off, sticks (cockleburr)** | Medium (carried) | Good — free ride, standard dispersal | Variable but viable |
| **Misses entirely (ballistic)** | Long (ballistic throw) | Adequate — no nutrient bed, but viable soil | Local (stays in vent zone) |
| **Wind-carried (passive)** | Very long (one-way) | **Poor** — increasingly hostile day-side conditions | Deteriorating |

The viper is the only high-range dispersal vector that keeps seeds in
viable habitat. Wind dispersal is actively hostile: surface flow goes
terminator → substellar, carrying seeds deeper into increasingly
inhospitable territory. A wind-riding seed drifts toward the substellar
hellscape with no return ticket.

**Volcanic resurfacing as habitat renewal:** Vent zones are
periodically resurfaced by lava flows — the volcanism that provides
CaF₂ also destroys existing thorn-flower populations. Freshly cooled
lava is premium real estate: new CaF₂ deposits, fresh mineral
substrate, zero competition. Pre-scattered seeds (embedded in nearby
corpses by vipers) are first to recolonize. Fire-ecology analog:
lodgepole pines need fire; thorn-flowers need eruptions. The volcanism
isn't a hazard to work around — it is the engine that keeps the system
cycling. The thorn-flower's relationship with its volcano is the same
as its relationship with its viper: the thing that destroys it is the
thing it needs most.

**4. Fluorine-Vipers**

**4.1 The Biological Rail Gun**

*\[CONFIRMED --- V2.3\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** The fluorine-viper does not manufacture its weapon. It
steals thorns from thorn-plants. The viper evolved: (1) silane tissue
immunity to HF, (2) extraction behavior to harvest thorns without
triggering them, (3) magazine anatomy to store stolen thorns intact, (4)
biological capacitors for energy storage, (5) electromagnetic rail-gun
launch mechanism.

**✓ RESOLVED:** The viper is a biological rail gun. It charges its
capacitors at thorn-flower charging stations (solar power,
tower-independent — §4.2), then uses electromagnetic acceleration to
fire stolen thorns at range. Not a biter. Not pneumatic.
Electromagnetic.

The magazine holds multiple thorns at different stages of readiness.
Sacrificial ammunition, continuously restocked by harvesting from
thorn-plant populations. The viper's metabolic investment is minimal
compared to growing its own thorns --- no SiC synthesis, no CaF₂
concentration, no acid production.

**4.2 Ecological Constraints**

*\[REVISED --- V2.3 --- tower-independent\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** The vipers need armed thorn-flowers for both ammunition

**4.3 Charging Behavior**

*\[REVISED --- V2.3 --- solar-powered\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** Vipers charge at thorn-flowers, drawing power stored in

A fully-charged viper is dangerous. A depleted viper that just fired its

Charging is when vipers are vulnerable --- stationary, predictable,

**✓ RESOLVED:** Vipers read the electrochromic display to select ambush

**5. Flash-Hoppers**

**5.1 The Detection Window**

*\[CONFIRMED --- V2.3 --- from chemistry thread\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** A railgun discharge is electromagnetically loud. The
current spike --- thousands of amps in milliseconds --- creates a
magnetic field pulse, broadband RF emission from accelerating charges,
and possible visible spark. This signature propagates at lightspeed. The
dart travels at \~200 m/s.

**✓ RESOLVED:** At 10 meters range: the dart takes 50 milliseconds to
arrive. The EM signature arrives in 33 nanoseconds. That's a 50 ms
warning window. An eternity for silicon neurology.

**5.2 The Organism**

**✓ RESOLVED:** The flash-hopper: small (fist-sized to head-sized),
because mass matters when you must accelerate laterally in under 50 ms.
Square-cube law in 1.82g brutally punishes large bodies trying to jink.

Fast-breeding, high mortality, high fecundity. The rabbit of the
volcanic provinces. Abundant, cognitively minimal (brain devoted to
reflex, not thought). Everyone eats it. It survives by being numerous
and quick.

**5.3 Adaptations**

**5.3.1 Tuned EM Receptors**

Not general-purpose sensing. Specific receptors optimized for the
spectral signature of railgun discharge --- the broadband RF crack of a
fast current transient. A matched filter: responds maximally to exactly
that waveform, ignores background grid hum and neural chatter.
Biological radar warning receiver.

**5.3.2 Hardwired Reflex Arc**

No cognition in the loop. Detection → motor response with absolute
minimum processing. EM receptor → threshold detector → motor neuron.
Three junctions. At semiconductor speeds: 0.1--1 ms neural delay. The
remaining 49 ms is available for physical movement.

The hopper doesn't decide to dodge. It cannot help dodging. The reflex
is involuntary. Consciousness, if present, experiences the dodge after
it's already happened.

**5.3.3 Pre-Tensioned Launch System**

Normal locomotion can't accelerate fast enough. Stored elastic energy
released all at once. Carbosilane-fiber spring tendons held under
tension by a molecular latch. Reflex signal releases the latch. Fibers
contract explosively. The hopper is hurled sideways --- a full
body-length in 30--40 ms.

Not jumping. Being catapulted by its own skeleton.

**5.3.4 Randomized Dodge Vector**

If it always jumped left, vipers would lead left. Direction determined
by chaotic/noisy process --- thermal noise in a semiconductor junction,
or free-running oscillator sampled at detection moment. True randomness.
The hopper doesn't know which way it will jump until it's already
jumping.

**5.4 The Vulnerability Window**

**✓ RESOLVED:** After firing, the hopper must re-tension the springs.
This takes seconds. During this recovery window: vulnerable. Can't dodge
again.

A viper timing a follow-up shot to the recovery period kills the hopper.
This creates selection pressure for faster re-tensioning, multiple
independent spring systems, or escape behaviors that don't rely on the
primary dodge reflex.

**6. The Arms Race**

**6.1 Counter-Adaptation Spiral**

*\[CONFIRMED --- V2.3\]*

**✓ RESOLVED:** Viper counter-adaptations drive hopper evolution and
vice versa:

Faster darts (shorter window) → faster reflexes. Closer ambush range →
better passive detection (sense viper before it fires). Multi-dart
volley → multi-direction dodge (tumble/spin). Coordinated pack fire →
threat triangulation (dodge away from centroid). Feint discharge → feint
discrimination.

**6.2 The Feint**

**✓ RESOLVED:** The feint is particularly nasty. The viper creates a
discharge-like EM pulse without firing --- partial current spike through
a dummy load. Hopper's reflex triggers. Hopper is mid-air, committed,
springs discharged. Viper fires for real. No escape.

**6.3 Feint Economics**

**✓ RESOLVED:** A full-power decoy costs a shot's worth of capacitor
energy --- defeating the purpose. The viper is hunting for the minimum
viable fake: the cheapest pulse that still triggers the reflex. The
hopper is evolutionarily trying to raise its discrimination threshold:
only dodge for pulses expensive enough to be real.

**The gradient:**

• Very cheap fake → easy to distinguish, hoppers ignore, worthless

• Moderately cheap → some hoppers dodge, marginal value

• Nearly-full-power → almost all hoppers dodge, but you've spent most of
a shot anyway

• Full power → all hoppers dodge, no net gain

**✓ RESOLVED:** Both species are locked in continuous negotiation over
where the discrimination threshold sits. The viper wants it low (cheap
fakes work). The hopper wants it high (only expensive pulses trigger
dodge). Every generation, the threshold shifts based on who's winning.

This is Batesian mimicry economics, but the viper is mimicking itself.

**6.4 Pattern Discrimination**

Hopper counter-evolution: the reflex becomes more discriminating. Learns
(evolutionarily) to distinguish real discharge from feint by subtler
signature features --- exact waveform shape, magnetic field from actual
dart acceleration, secondary signatures from rail heating. The threshold
detector becomes a pattern classifier.

Both species are driven toward increasingly sophisticated EM signal
processing. The hopper is evolving biological electronic warfare
capabilities. The viper is evolving signature management.

**7.2 Fitness Gradient Dynamics**

Over geological time, the lineages that avoid vents entirely don't die

Selection pressure doesn't require dramatic mortality events. It just

**7.3 Equilibrium**

**✓ RESOLVED:** The vent zone is not a death trap with corpses piled

cannot be too successful or they exterminate their own prey base.

The system balances on reproductive fitness gradients:

• Prey visit often enough to stay competitive

• Vipers take enough to sustain themselves

• Thorn-plants get enough seed-dispersal to maintain the armed
population

• Nobody exterminates anybody

This is how real ecosystems work. Not dramatic confrontations, but
statistical gradients playing out over geological time.
