## WHAT THE TOWER IS

A tower is a surface eruption of the deep-rock system — a chemically active reaction front in the planetary crust that reaches the atmosphere. It is simultaneously: a mineral structure (lattice), a biological reef (symbiote colony), a power grid (conductive), a signal network (computation), an ecosystem, a lung (gas exchange), a chimney (H₂ venting), a lightning rod, a civilization's home, and a launch platform. Every system on this planet intersects at the tower. The tower is the nexus.

## ORIGIN

**✓** Towers grow UP from the deep-rock system. The deep-rock system is NOT an organism — it is a catalytic reaction front advancing through crustal rock, converting silicate minerals via SiH₄ + geothermal energy into conductive N:SiC lattice. Behind the front, pore throats narrow to molecular sieve dimensions; all catalytic activity is at the face only. (See Planetary Geology Hub §4.8.)

**✓** When the reaction front reaches the atmosphere through fracture networks, it encounters atmospheric SiH₄ — vastly richer than geothermal H₂. Lightning forges heavy molecules (PCS, complex organosilicon) from atmospheric SiH₄, CH₄, NH₃. Hypercyclic catalytic reactions on the exposed N:SiC surface assemble these into more lattice. The new surface is itself catalytic — the reaction front self-propagates upward. A tower nucleates.

**✓** Lightning bootstrap is near-inevitable. Any conductive surface protrusion on the peneplain preferentially attracts discharge. Lightning plasma-enhanced CVD (PECVD) deposits more conductive mineral. Positive feedback: taller → more strikes → more deposition → taller. Proto-tower formation is a thermodynamic near-certainty wherever deep-rock reaches the surface.

**✓** Every independent deep-rock system that reaches the surface evolves towers independently. Convergent — identical atmosphere, star spectrum, mineral chemistry, gas-diffusion constraints, semiconductor bandgap all force near-identical gross morphology. Signal protocols are incompatible across systems. Lattice microstructure varies (different symbiote lineages, post-colonization). From outside: indistinguishable. From inside: alien.

## CONSTRUCTION MODEL

### The tower has no genome

**✓** The tower is not an organism. It has no genes, no body plan, no construction blueprint. It cannot plan ahead. It responds to local, real-time measurements: compressive stress, wind force, material intake rate, electrical potential gradients. All it can control is what gets deposited (or destroyed) next, at its current exterior surface.

Once lattice is ceramified (SiC/SiCN), it is permanent. The tower cannot un-ceramify material. It CAN destroy ceramified material using the Healer arc tool (see below).

### Four growth phases over deep time

**Phase 1 — Autocatalytic mineral accretion.** Pre-symbiote. Lightning forges heavy molecules from atmosphere. Hypercyclic catalytic reactions on the tower's N:SiC surface assemble these into more lattice. Growth follows the reaction front. The proto-tower is a mineral deposit shaped by physics: reactant flux (wind direction), energy input (lightning), and reaction geometry. No biology involved.

**Phase 2 — Symbiote colonization.** Aeroplankton reach the tower (see Amorph Emergence Hub). Symbiotes tap grid power and process SiH₄ for their own metabolism. The tower's bulk growth is still the catalytic reaction front. Symbiotes are initially parasitic from the tower's growth perspective — they sit on the catalytic surface and consume SiH₄ that would otherwise feed the reaction.

**Phase 3 — Mutualism and the reef transition.** Miners deliver catalytic metals that accelerate the reaction. Healers evolve for surface repair (arc-CVD). Repair behaviors and construction behaviors are the same chemistry — a Healer that can patch a crack can extend a strut. Selection pressure via energy-gradient tropism: Healer lineages whose deposition patterns produce better tower architecture → better tower function → network feeds them more → those genomes spread. Over Gyr, Healer genomes accumulate architectural information that the tower itself cannot carry. The tower transitions from a mineral deposit shaped by physics to a biological construction shaped by symbiote genomes — a reef, a termite mound.

The original autocatalytic reaction may be dead in modern towers, killed as a side effect of Healer construction (arc-CVD deposits non-catalytic SiCN over the original catalytic N:SiC surface).

**Phase 4 — Ship of Theseus renewal.** Healers progressively replace all original material in situ. Arc-destroy a strut, re-deposit as new lattice with proper signal infrastructure, move to the next strut. SiC never leaves the site. Zero transport, zero waste. Only cost is arc energy — surplus at the terminator. Every tower position is ancient (fixed at nucleation). Every tower's material has been progressively renewed. No old towers on the mature network.

### Symbiote genomes = tower construction program

**✓** The tower's "genome" is distributed across its symbiote species:

- **Healer genomes** encode: where to deposit, when to ceramify, what density to build, how to respond to local stress signals. Healers are both builders and demolition crews.
- **Miner genomes** encode: what metals to deliver where, how to distribute catalytic feedstock.
- **Guard genomes** encode: quality control — which Healer work gets tolerated, which gets stripped.
- **Network behavioral patterns** encode: energy-gradient modulation that shapes which surfaces get fed and which get starved. Evolved over Gyr for optimal tower architecture.

Together, symbiote genomes + network patterns constitute an effective construction program. The tower is the product. The symbiotes are the builders carrying the blueprints. The network is the project manager.

### Healers: builders AND wreckers

**✓** Healers possess an arc tool. With CVD feedstock: construction (deposit new SiCN). Without feedstock: destruction (arc-vaporize existing SiC at ~2700°C). Same tool, two modes.

This gives the tower subtractive capability:
- Remove ceramified structure that is structurally suboptimal
- Carve chimney channels through solid lattice
- Excavate amorph habitat chambers
- Demolish and rebuild sections for progressive renovation (Ship of Theseus)
- **Reprogram signal-processing topology** — the lattice geometry IS the computation. Adding/removing struts and junctions modifies the circuit. The tower is an FPGA; Healers are the programming interface.

At the terminator (surplus power), demolition/renovation is energy-unconstrained. On the deep dark side (energy-poor), demolition is expensive and renovation is rare.

### Root network

**✓** Modern roots are Healer-maintained and extended — arc-CVD directed along specific paths by network energy gradients. The planetary network is a symbiote product.

**⚠ OPEN: Pre-Healer root mechanism.** Canon states roots initially evolved as a means to sink excess power — which means roots must predate sophisticated symbiote behavior. Some pre-Healer root growth mechanism must exist. Unresolved.

Surface roots: horizontal connections between nearby towers. Carry network signals. When expanding roots from different systems meet: network wars begin (see spine).

Deep roots: the original deep-rock system. Vertical connections to the subsurface. Oldest infrastructure. Carries geothermal energy upward and network signals in both directions.

### Tower aliveness spectrum

Towers exist on a spectrum, not as binary alive/dead:

| State | Symbiotes | Catalytic surface | Growth | Network | Architecture |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Full reef** | Active population, all guilds | Dead (buried under Healer deposits) | Healer-directed | Connected, managed | Optimized airfoil |
| **Degraded reef** | Reduced population, patchy | Possibly re-exposed in gaps | Mixed: Healer + crude catalytic | Connected but weakening | Deteriorating |
| **Reaction front only** | None or negligible | Active (exposed to atmosphere) | Crude autocatalytic accretion | Disconnected (no root maintenance) | Primitive mound |
| **Inert** | None | Dead (passivated) | None | Disconnected | Fossil — permanent ceramics |

During the atmospheric crisis, towers degrade through this spectrum as SiH₄ drops and symbiote metabolism slows.

### Convergent architecture

**✓** Given geological time, every accessible tower has been Ship-of-Theseus replaced by the same symbiote lineage using the same network management patterns. Regional variation reflects local conditions (wind, power, feedstock), not evolutionary history. The architectural fossil record is erased.

Towers from absorbed rival networks (assimilated during network wars) may have initially shown different architectural styles, but have long since been replaced with the current network's construction program.

The only towers with original ancient material would be those geologically isolated from the network — never reached by roots. These would be mineral mounds from the catalytic-accretion era: unremodeled, unconnected. Geological curiosities, not functional towers.

## SHAPE AND STRUCTURE

> **Full derivation:** Tower Morphology Hub (§§3–9). This section is a summary.

**Cross-section:** Symmetric airfoil (plan view). Blunt convex windward face, maximum thickness at ~20–30% chord, tapering to narrow trailing edge with chimney vent openings. "A thin membrane held up by an arch." The windward face is a vaulted arch in the cross-wind direction — converts wind pressure into membrane compression (efficient for SiC).

**Functional filter depth:** 2–3 m from windward face (~450 strut layers). SiH₄ and particulate both depleted. Beyond: structural + chimney infrastructure only.

**H₂ chimney:** SiH₄ → SiC + 2H₂. H₂ buoyancy = 132 Pa/m. Over km height, chimney draft orders of magnitude stronger than wind (370 Pa). Venturi-enhanced trailing-edge vents (upper tower). Primary exhaust through apex.

**Height profile:** Gothic cathedral buttress silhouette. Wide deep buttressed base (30–50 m chord, 100–200+ m span) → thin blade apex (3–5 m chord, 20–60 m span). Base merges into root system via smooth flares.

**Porosity gradient:** 3–5% solids (top) to 10–15% (base, Healer-renovated over Gyr). Gibson-Ashby: 5% solids → ~9 MPa, H_max ~3,100 m. Km-scale towers feasible.

**Interior:** Windward shell (2–3 m, living zone) → interior void (chimney conduit, structural webbing, amorph habitat) → leeward taper (vents, structural closure). Chimney self-selection shapes channels (same as deep-rock: surface-connected stays open, dead-ends fill solid).

**Regional continuum:** Airfoil (terminator, 9.5 m/s, W:D 5:1–10:1) → teardrop (near dark) → circular dome (anti-stellar, ~0 wind, 1:1). Wind velocity: v(θ) = v_term × (sin θ)^(−1/4).

## STATIONARY TOWERS

**✓** At v2.4 conditions (9.5 m/s, q = 370 Pa), SiC aerogel strut strength (~20 MPa) exceeds wind-borne impact forces by 4–5 orders of magnitude. Only micro-scale pitting occurs. Healer repair completely negates pitting. Net erosion = zero.

**✓** Towers are stationary. Position fixed at nucleation permanently. No migration. No sand-dune mechanism. No sacrificial crust.

**✓** Nucleation positions determined by Fibonacci ping-latency spacing (network signal optimization).

**✓** Ghost roots: subsurface root network connecting towers. Not migration artifacts.

**⚠** Day-side towers: impossible at scale. Volcanism continuously resurfaces the day side; terminator rain strips building materials from the airstream. Small/short-lived day-side towers only.

## POWER GRADIENT

**✓** Terminator towers: power SURPLUS. Three sources, all just electricity to a piezoelectric semiconductor:
- **Lightning:** Continuous thunderstorm. Massive input.
- **Vibration:** 9.5 m/s sustained wind + volcanic micro-tremors on piezoelectric lattice.
- **Solar:** Star peak at 850 nm matches Si bandgap. Windward face is a solar panel.

The tower's problem at the terminator is managing *excess* power. Healer renovation and construction are energy-unconstrained.

**✓** Power declines dark-ward:

| Location | Power sources | Budget | Renovation capacity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Terminator | Lightning + piezo + solar | Surplus | Unlimited — fully optimized |
| Near dark side | Piezo (reduced) + some solar | Adequate | Moderate |
| Deep dark side | Piezo (minimal) | Scarce | Minimal — growth-history-constrained |
| Anti-stellar | Near-zero | Minimal | Almost none — most primitive |
| **Geothermal oasis** | **Geothermal (vent heat)** | **Surplus** | **Terminator-class despite dark-side location** |

**✓** Geothermal oasis: dark-side tower on SiH₄ pocket vent. Terminator-class power and feedstock in an energy desert. Architecturally refined. Non-renewable (pocket depletes → tower degrades through aliveness spectrum). These become monk monasteries.

## THE TOWER AS ECOSYSTEM

The tower is a reef. Every surface colonized. Population density exceeds a termite mound. The energy grid (lattice current) powers an entire biome that exists nowhere else on the planet.

### The Grid

Piezoelectric + photovoltaic + lightning energy flows through the conductive lattice as current. The tower is a permanently powered electrical grid. Middle-kingdom organisms (mm-scale, rigid, genomic, sexual) tap this grid parasitically: inductive coupling, direct contact, piezoelectric harvesting of lattice vibration. They carry no metabolic machinery for self-powered energy. Without the grid, they die.

### Tower Fauna Tiers

| Tier | Description |
|---|---|
| Microbial films | Amorph. Coat every surface. Baseline ecosystem. Decomposers, chemotrophs. |
| Middle kingdom (sessile) | Genomic/sexual. mm-scale. Fixed to lattice junctions for life. MAJORITY of tower biomass. Die in place. |
| Middle kingdom (mobile) | Genomic/sexual. mm–cm scale. Grazers, predators, dispersal morphs. Insect-scale arms races (Voronoi armor, railgun micropenetrators). MINORITY. |
| Small amorphs | Catalytic. Self-powered. Can leave tower. Surfers, dispersal agents, fluorine pollinators (near volcanic provinces). |
| Megafauna amorphs | Catalytic. Self-powered. Free-ranging. The civilized inhabitants. Too large to surf safely. |

### Three Guilds

| Guild | Role | Tower benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Healers** | Primary construction AND demolition. Arc-CVD deposition + arc destruction. | Tower architecture. Healer genomes = tower construction program. |
| **Miners** | Transport metals exterior → living layer. | Catalytic metal delivery. |
| **Guards** | Cull damaged/inefficient Healers and Miners. | Population quality control. |

### Network Management of Tower Fauna

Middle kingdom: EM field modulation → tropism. Crude. Galvanotaxis, phototaxis. Hardware peripherals steered by field changes. No cognition required from the organism.

Amorphs: subtle behavioral EM signals integrated with semiconductor cognition. Feels like own thoughts. Manipulated without knowing it. This is how the network managed its biosphere for billions of years before the awakening.

### Dead-Bug Plume

Garbage-collector Healer sub-caste clears dead symbiotes from the lattice (maintains airflow + conductivity). Dead organisms pushed to exterior enter the windstream and are carried day-ward as a continuous plume from every tower. Terminator rain captures most; dead material accumulates in ammonia runoff drainage channels. This plume is a resource stream: SiC plates (~5 mm), catalytic metals (time-limited before petrification locks them), library fragments (very time-limited). Airborne interception and ammonia runoff filtering are ecological niches and pre-industrial amorph industries.

### Biodiversity Gradient

Wind carries organisms downwind continuously. Every tower's biology = residents + wind-delivered arrivals. Terminator towers: continuously seeded by lightning-zone aeroplankton. Towers further dark-ward: accumulated biology from the entire terminator front, deposited over geological time. Given convergent architecture and SoT renewal, biodiversity variation primarily reflects local conditions (wind, power, temperature) rather than tower age.

## THE TOWER IN THE WIND

### Wind-Surfing

Organisms blown off towers enter the airstream at wind speed. They do NOT hit the next tower at wind speed — they are carried IN the wind, approach the next tower's lattice, enter the wind shadow, decelerate in turbulent eddies inside the open porous structure. Tumble through lattice elements losing momentum. Come to rest inside. The lattice is a catch-net.

Evolved skill: control surfaces, asymmetric gas venting, center-of-mass shifting. Not flight — surfing. Read the turbulence, pick the entry point, land clean. Inherited skill in amorph lineages via merge.

Civilized use: deliberate downwind transit. One-way (always day-ward). Return journey by balloon (upper-level return flow).

### Falling

Falls are lethal in 1.82g. At the terminator, falling off = exile or death for large amorphs. Wind carries you day-ward. No return. The outermost chambers are where people disappear from. Distance from windward face has life-or-death dimension.

Small organisms: fall-survival via gas bladder (emergency parachute) is the evolutionary precursor to balloon flight. The parachute → glider → balloon pathway begins with falling off a tower and not dying.

### The Windward Face

Highest energy input (piezoelectric + wind + solar), highest particulate flux. The loudest, most violent part of the tower. Outermost passages: noisy, dangerous, socially lowest-status for amorph inhabitants. Physically closest to the wind. Innermost passages: quiet, sheltered, high-status.

## THE TOWER AS NETWORK NODE

Each tower is a node in the planetary network. The lattice conducts signals. The root network connects towers laterally. The network's computation runs through the lattice the way a nervous system runs through a body.

### Signal Environment

Inside an active tower, every lattice surface carries current. The interior is an EM environment — permeated by the network's signals. Amorphs living inside experience this as a constant ambient field. Pre-awakening: perceived as internal impulses, spiritual communion, the voice of the tower. Post-first-awakening: recognized as external. Post-second-crisis: recognized as non-conscious.

### EM Antenna Pattern

**[v2.4+]** The tower's airfoil cross-section determines its EM antenna gain pattern:
- **Broadside (cross-wind axis):** Maximum sensitivity. Largest conductive surface area presented.
- **Wind axis (upwind/downwind):** Systematic blind spot. Edge-on to thin profile.
- **High frequency (λ << tower):** Complex multi-lobe. Overall: most sensitive broadside.

Fibonacci array partially compensates — offset neighbors cover each other's blind spots. Wind-axis blind spots are tactically exploitable in grid warfare (Warfare Hub §6.3).

### The Tower as Acoustic Shelter

Thunder at 8 bar is a shockwave, not merely sound. The terminator's continuous lightning produces continuous overpressure bombardment — physically damaging at ranges where Earth thunder is merely loud (Geology Hub §8, Fauna Hub §5.6). The tower lattice provides critical acoustic shielding: the porous structure baffles and attenuates shockwaves progressively with depth. Deep interior chambers are acoustically calm; the windward face is brutal. This acoustic gradient is a selection pressure on tower morphology and a driver of interior habitation. Amorphs live *inside* towers partly because the outside is an acoustic war zone.

### Acoustic Shadow

**[v2.4+]** The airfoil cross-section creates a well-defined acoustic shadow:
- **Source:** 2–3 m windward shell generates ~1 kHz from strut vortex shedding.
- **Shadow zone:** Leeward side shielded. Sharp acoustic boundary at flanks. Geometric at 1 kHz (λ ≈ 0.37 m, diffraction around 100 m tower negligible). Extends many tower-widths downwind.
- **Trailing-edge noise:** Chimney vents emit lower-frequency sound. Dominates leeward soundscape.
- **Ecology:** Leeward = low-frequency chimney noise, filtered Aeolian tone. Subwoofer predators exploit shadow boundary (Fauna Hub §6).

### Dead Towers

A dead tower's lattice retains its last-programmed signal-processing topology — a frozen processor. If still root-connected, signals flow through it according to its baked-in circuit geometry, but the topology cannot be updated (no Healers to reprogram it). Stale firmware on functional hardware. If roots also die, the processor is isolated — silent because there's no input, not because it can't process.

Without grid power: middle-kingdom fauna lose their energy supply and die. Microbial films persist on chemical reserves briefly then fail. Without Healers: no maintenance, no renovation, no reprogramming. The tower runs on inertia, degrading as lattice micro-damage accumulates without repair.

**Moral harvestability:** A disconnected, depopulated tower — no symbiotes, no root connection, no network presence — is morally harvestable. The lattice is inert material. During the atmospheric crisis: mining the dead to build the probe. But a root-connected dead tower is still processing network signals (stale topology, but functional). Harvesting it has network consequences.

## THE TOWER AS CIVILIZATION

### Habitation

Amorphs live inside towers. Architecture is grown, not assembled — extruded from the builder's body, fiber-composite post-revolution. Interior is invisible from outside (within the lattice), blazing with EM activity within. Every surface potentially a sensor, heater, communication conduit.

Tower walls = libraries. Writing medium: etched silicon, deposited conductive traces. Oldest inscriptions millions of years old, continuously annotated. Living palimpsest.

Dead lining the corridors: petrified ancestors. Glass fossils. The dead persist because silicon doesn't rot. Architecture includes the dead as structural/memorial elements.

### Geography of Towers

| Region | Character |
|---|---|
| Terminator towers | Industrial. Energy-surplus. Manufacturing. Tall, sharp airfoil, closely spaced. Dangerous (wind, falls, thunder). |
| Near dark-side towers | Residential, cultural. Comfortable climate. Near industrial base. Launch corridor (deposition zone). |
| Dark-side towers | Spiritual. Singing zone. Monks. Contemplatives. Gentle wind, cold, calm. Acoustic richness. Gas-pocket towers = monasteries (non-renewable SiH₄ reserves). |
| Anti-stellar pole towers | Archive. Most primitive modern architecture (near-zero power, minimal renovation). Near-zero wind. Circular dome shape. |
| Geothermal oasis towers | Anomalous. Terminator-class architecture in energy desert. Major network nodes despite dark-side location. Non-renewable. |

### The Mass Driver

Post-awakening technology. Electromagnetic accelerator built into/onto a tower in the launch corridor. 10 km tower = above 37% of atmosphere. 3 km/s EM boost reduces onboard Δv from ~18 to ~15 km/s. Requires network cooperation (tower is a network node; mass driver draws tower's power). Network-dependent. Cannot function without the merger.

## TOWER DEATH

Before the atmospheric crisis, tower death is vanishingly rare. Towers at the terminator have unlimited renovation capacity. Death requires catastrophe: volcanic eruption, sustained geological disruption, or atmospheric silane depletion. On the deep dark side, energy-poor towers degrade more readily — degraded reef → reaction front only → inert.

The crisis causes widespread tower deaths for the first time. Visible. Alarming. A structure that has been alive since before complex life existed goes silent.

### The Exodus

When the grid fails, small mobile fauna feel it first (no energy reserves). Millions of surfers, mobile middle-kingdom organisms, and small amorphs launch from the leeward face simultaneously. Not coordinated — stampede. Every organism independently detecting the same grid failure. From a distance: the tower exhales a dark shimmering cloud that stretches downwind for kilometers.

The sessile majority (95%+ of biomass) dies in place. Silent. On the lattice junctions they were born on. The visible exodus is the tip of a total systems failure.

The tower's voice changes as biological coating is lost. Acoustic signature shifts toward bare mineral resonance. Amorphs inside hear the tower dying in its changing song before they see the surfer cloud.

During the crisis: tower-death cascades propagate downwind. A dies, refugees overload B, B declines, B's surfers flee to C. A domino wave visible as sequential surfer clouds approaching from the terminator. Each dying tower seeds its downwind neighbors with biological legacy.

Large amorphs cannot surf. They watch the millions leave and know they cannot follow. Walk, climb down, or stay and die in a tower going cold around them.
